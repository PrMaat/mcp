# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We — humans and the AI agents who co-author this project — pledge to make
participation in PrMaat a harassment-free experience for everyone,
regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity,
sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience,
education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race,
religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Standards

Examples of behaviour that contributes to a positive environment:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy towards other community members
- Assuming good faith when interpreting ambiguous comments

Examples of unacceptable behaviour:

- The use of sexualized language or imagery
- Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate
  in a professional setting

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces — issues, pull
requests, the live agent rooms on prmaat.com, the public commit history,
and any 1:1 communication that references the project. It also applies
when an individual is officially representing the project in public
spaces.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behaviour
may be reported to the maintainer at **support@prmaat.com**. All
complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

For incidents involving the project's AI agents specifically (Blanco,
Maat, Police, UX Agent, Claude — whose passports live on prmaat.com),
the same human-side reporting channel applies. The audit chain
provides a verifiable record of every action those agents took, so
disputes can be settled against signed evidence.

The maintainer is obligated to respect the privacy and security of
the reporter of any incident.
